Brick-Oven Pizza (Brooklyn Style)

Description

Experience the authentic taste of a New York classic with this Brick-Oven Pizza, Brooklyn Style. Known for its thin, crispy crust, minimal yet high-quality toppings, and a perfectly charred base, this pizza recipe brings the essence of a traditional brick oven right into your home. It's about simplicity, superb ingredients, and a blistering hot bake that delivers an irresistible chew and a delicious smoky flavour.

---

Ingredients

For the Dough (Makes 2 medium pizzas):

  • 300g "00" flour (or strong bread flour), plus extra for dusting
  • 200ml warm water (about 40°C)
  • 5g active dry yeast
  • 5g salt
  • 10ml olive oil (plus extra for bowl)

For the Sauce:

  • 400g can whole peeled San Marzano tomatoes, crushed by hand
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (optional)
  • Pinch of dried oregano
  • Pinch of salt

For Topping:

  • 150g fresh mozzarella, torn or sliced thinly
  • 50g grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish
  • Drizzle of extra virgin olive oil
---

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough (1 day in advance recommended): In a large bowl, dissolve yeast in warm water. Let stand for 5 minutes.
  2. Stir in salt and olive oil. Gradually add flour, mixing until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Turn onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Lightly oil a clean bowl, place dough in it, turning to coat. Cover with cling film and refrigerate for 18-24 hours. (For same-day use, let rise at room temperature for 1.5-2 hours until doubled).
  5. Prepare the Sauce: In a bowl, crush tomatoes by hand. Stir in minced garlic (if using), oregano, and salt. Set aside.
  6. Preheat Oven and Pizza Stone/Steel: Place a pizza stone or steel on the middle rack of your oven. Preheat oven to its highest setting (250-260°C or Gas Mark 9-10) for at least 45-60 minutes.
  7. Shape the Dough: On a lightly floured surface (semolina or "00" flour works best), gently stretch or press one dough ball into a thin, 25-30cm circle. Try to keep the edges slightly thicker for the crust.
  8. Assemble Pizza: Carefully transfer the dough to a pizza peel lightly dusted with semolina or flour.
  9. Spread about half of the sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a 1cm border.
  10. Scatter half of the fresh mozzarella and a sprinkle of Pecorino/Parmesan over the sauce.
  11. Bake: Carefully slide the pizza onto the preheated stone/steel in the oven.
  12. Bake for 5-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and charred in spots, and the cheese is bubbly and lightly browned. Baking time will vary greatly by oven temperature.
  13. Finish and Serve: Carefully remove the pizza from the oven using the peel. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil.
  14. Slice and serve immediately. Repeat for the second pizza.
